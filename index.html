Playing for Keeps

Well, folks, it’s here. 2020. What a hell of a time to be alive. On the one hand, it’s a marvel. In comparison to the generations that preceded us, we live in rare comfort. I mean, look at us. Radio waves connect our little electronic boxes and transmit the shit we talk to one another in the blink of an eye. We watch baseball wherever and whenever we want. For the most part, we have jobs and healthcare. None of us live on the street.

Yet, for all the modern world has given us, trouble looms. In our lifetimes, global capitals, like ours, will be abandoned or radically transformed to cope with a rapidly changing climate. Animal species across the globe are hurtling towards extinction. Private companies know more about us than our best friends, girlfriends or spouses. Three people own more wealth than the bottom 50 percent of Americans. War rages on.

It’s all too much. Personally, I can’t escape the thought that I’m breathing in a year that, for better or worse, will be seen as a turning point in human history. This year’s election, in particular, is sure to reverberate through the centuries. The heaviness of the moment is paralyzing. We could all use some levity, which is why I’m so fucking juiced about another year — decade! — of PFK.

Think about it. We have a full season of baseball, six months of surprise breakouts, long-awaited debuts and Sunday meltdowns, all before a single vote is cast in the November elections.

If there were ever a year to welcome reliable nonsense and fantasy, it’s this fucking year. I expect much silliness. It’s my only requirement.

Your new commish,

Spencer
