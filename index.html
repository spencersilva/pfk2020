Playing for Keeps

February 26, 2020 — Election Results

After years of partisan gridlock, the dam has broken.

On Tuesday, February 25, PFK managers voted resoundingly for a suite of modest reforms: a new category (SV+H), a new keeper deadline (August 31) and an extra roster spot.

How did it go down? By all accounts, over the past few seasons, a few liberal hairs have wandered up the right conservative asses and thereby fortified a new, if shaky, center-left PFK majority — the first in a generation.

“It’s a great day for PFK,” said PFK commissioner Spencer Silva, whose Jose Bautista Party authored two of the league’s three new measures. “I was getting really tired of drafting closers in the first- and second-rounds of the draft.”

Meanwhile, the conservative three-member Tea-Ball Party was united in opposition to each measure. Yet despite the undesired result, conservative leader Bobby Ray was upbeat about some of the changes.

“I’m sure I’ll be stoked on Holds once I find a sick middle reliever whose box scores I look forward to every day!” Ray said.

The final tally:

  • DICKROD: Aye (20%), Nay (80%)
    • BARB: Aye (70%), Nay (30%)
    • Roster Expansion: Aye (70%), Nay (30%)
    • New Keeper Deadline: Aye (70%), Nay (30%)

Trades

In other news, there were a few offseason trades that came together Tuesday evening.

  1. Mutilation traded Corey Seager to the Bubblers in exchange for Corey Kluber.
  2. Bubblers traded his German Marquez and Giancarlo Stanton for Problematic Faves’s Josh Hader and recently-acquired Mike Soroka.

Good luck, gents

-Commish

January 29, 2020

Well, folks, it’s here. 2020. What a hell of a time to be alive. On the one hand, it’s a marvel. In comparison to the generations that preceded us, we live in rare comfort. I mean, look at us. Radio waves connect our little electronic boxes and transmit the shit we talk to one another in the blink of an eye. We watch baseball wherever and whenever we want. For the most part, we have jobs and healthcare. None of us live on the street.

Yet, for all the modern world has given us, trouble looms. In our lifetimes, global capitals, like ours, will be abandoned or radically transformed to cope with a rapidly changing climate. Animal species across the globe are hurtling towards extinction. Private companies know more about us than our best friends, girlfriends or spouses. Three people own more wealth than the bottom 50 percent of Americans. War rages on.

It’s all too much. Personally, I can’t escape the thought that I’m breathing in a year that, for better or worse, will be seen as a turning point in human history. This year’s election, in particular, is sure to reverberate through the centuries. The heaviness of the moment is paralyzing. We could all use some levity, which is why I’m so fucking juiced about another year — decade! — of PFK.

Think about it. We have a full season of baseball, six months of surprise breakouts, long-awaited debuts and Sunday meltdowns, all before a single vote is cast in the November elections. If there were ever a year to welcome reliable nonsense and fantasy, it’s this fucking year. I expect much silliness. It’s my only requirement.

Your new commish,

Spencer
